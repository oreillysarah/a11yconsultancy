Is your website accessible to people with disabilities and to older people? About 15% of the world's population lives with some form of disability with this estimate rising due to the global population ageing (WHO, 2019).Web Accessibility can help people with different types of disabilities access sites e.g. people with sight difficulties (or who are blind and use a screen reader to interpret information), people with intellectual disabilities, people with physical disabilities and hearing disabilities. It can also benefit people without disabilities such as non-native language speakers and people who have limited time to find information on a site.

Key Advantages of Accessibility for your business

  1. Enhance Your Brand
  2. Extend Market Reach: The global market of people with disabilities is over 1 billion people with a spending power of more than $6 trillion.
  3. Improve SEO (Search engine optimization ratings)
  4. Legal Risk: Many countries have laws requiring digital accessibility, and the issue is of increased legal concern. E.g. EN 301 549 (European Standard), or the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (UK) which require WCAG 2.1 AA standard.
